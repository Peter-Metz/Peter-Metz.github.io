<h2 align="center">About</h2>

<h1 align="center">Everything we incubate is open source</h1>

<p style="text-align:justify">Since 2013, the Open Source Policy Center has incubated tools like models and webapps for predicting the outcomes of public policy. We identify tools that have the potential to fundamentally improve the voter and policymaker decisionmaking, and we help tool builders create open source projects that are professional, user oriented, and welcoming to outside technical contributors.</p>
<div style="text-align:justify">
The projects in our <a style="display:inline-block" href="/#portfolio">portfolio</a> have been supported staffers from Democratic and Republican Administrations, committees and subcommittees in Congress, Congressional leadership offices, and rank and file member offices, as well as national newspapers, primary and general election presidential campaigns, and dozens of university classes.
</div>
<br>
<p align="justify">In addition to incubating models, we are committed to helping our users by convening the open source community and educating policymakers, modelers, and the public about open source policy analysis.</p> 

<p align="justify">Please sign up for our newsletter to learn about useful models, events, and resources for public policy decisionmaking.</p>

<p align="left">Director</p>
